<h3>Indefinite articles</h3>
<p>The German indefinite articles for the subject of a sentence are <strong>ein</strong> and <strong>eine</strong>, and both mean <em>a</em> or <em>an</em>. <strong>Ein</strong> is used with masculine and neuter nouns, and <strong>eine</strong> is used with feminine nouns.</p>
<p>Example:</p>
<p><strong>Masculine nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>ein Arzt
<ul>
<li><em> a doctor</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>ein Fernsehapparat
<ul>
<li><em> a television</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Feminine nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>eine Tante
<ul>
<li><em> an aunt</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>eine Tochter
<ul>
<li><em> a daughter</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Neuter nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>ein Buch
<ul>
<li><em> a book</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>ein Bild
<ul>
<li><em> a picture</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
