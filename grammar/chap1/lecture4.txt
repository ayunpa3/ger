<h3>Indefinite articles</h3>
<p>The indefinite articles are known as <strong>ein</strong>-words. Another such <strong>ein</strong>-word is <strong>kein</strong>, which means <em>not any</em> or <em>no</em>. Notice that its ending in the feminine is identical to the ending required with <strong>eine</strong>.</p>
<p>Example:</p>
<p><strong>Masculine nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>kein Tisch
<ul>
<li><em>no table</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>kein Film
<ul>
<li><em>no film</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Feminine nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>keine Butter
<ul>
<li><em>no butter</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>keine Zeit
<ul>
<li><em>no time</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Neuter nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>kein Geld
<ul>
<li><em>no money</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>kein Schlafzimmer
<ul>
<li><em>no bedroom</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
