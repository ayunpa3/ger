<p>A noun illustrates its gender by being accompanied by either <strong>der</strong>, <strong>die</strong>, or <strong>das</strong>.</p>
<p>Example:</p>
<p><strong>Masculine nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Der Mann ist hier.
<ul>
<li><em>The man is here.</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Der Lehrer ist hier.
<ul>
<li><em>The teacher is here.</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Feminine nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Die Frau ist da.
<ul>
<li><em>The woman is there.</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Die Blume ist da.
<ul>
<li><em>The flower is there.</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Neuter nouns</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Das Auto ist klein.
<ul>
<li><em>The car is little.</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Das Haus ist klein.
<ul>
<li><em>The house is little.</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
